🫀 What is Cardiovascular Risk?

Cardiovascular risk refers to the likelihood of developing heart-related problems such as heart attack, stroke, or other vascular conditions. Key risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, obesity, smoking, unhealthy diet, and physical inactivity.

Blood Pressure

High blood pressure increases your risk of heart disease and stroke.

Cholesterol

High cholesterol can lead to artery blockage and heart problems.

Lifestyle

Exercise regularly, eat healthy, and avoid smoking to reduce risks.
